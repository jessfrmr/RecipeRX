Headache Relief

Curated recipes with the goal to relieve your headache

The Power of Natural Remedies

Headaches can stem from various factors, such as tension, dehydration, dietary triggers, or even stress. Each of our curated recipes has been carefully crafted to incorporate ingredients known for their potential to alleviate headaches and promote well-being.

Spinach + Almond Salad

This salad is packed with magnesium from spinach and healthy fats from almonds, which can help relax blood vessels and reduce headache symptoms.

Spinach + Almond Salad
Ingredients:
  • 1 cup fresh spinach
  • 1/2 cup slivered almonds
  • 2 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/4 cup sliced parmesan
  • 1/4 cup pomegranate seeds
Directions:
  1. In a small bowl, mix balsamic vinegar, olive oil, and salt and pepper to taste
  2. In another bowl, add the spinach and mix in your balsamic dressing
  3. Top with sliced parmesan, almonds, and pomegranate seeds. Enjoy!

Quinoa + Blueberry Breakfast Bowl

Packed with magnesium and antioxidants from fresh blueberries, this nourishing breakfast can help relax blood vessels and reduce the frequency and severity of headaches, particularly migraines.

Quinoa + Blueberry Breakfast Bowl
Ingredients:
  • 1/2 cup cooked quinoa
  • 1/2 cup fresh blueberries
  • 2 tablespoons Greek yogurt
  • 1/4 cup chopped walnuts
Directions:
  1. Cook quinoa according to package instructions and let cool
  2. In a bowl, layer the quinoa, fresh blueberries, and Greek yogurt
  3. Drizzle with honey or almond butter. Top with berries and shaved almonds as a final touch.
  4. Mix together before enjoying your delicious and riboflavin-rich breakfast bowl!

Tumeric + Honey Tea

Turmeric contains curcumin, which has anti-inflammatory properties and can help reduce headache-related pain and discomfort.

Tumeric + Honey Tea
Ingredients:
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp ground tumeric
  • a pinch of freshly ground pepper
  • 2 tsps honey
Directions:
  1. In a small pot, add water, tumeric, lemon juice and black pepper
  2. Bring to a boil, then turn the heat down and simmer for 10 minutes
  3. Take off the heat, add the honey and let cool for 2 minutes
  4. Pour the tea into a mug through a strainer to remove the black pepper and enjoy!
