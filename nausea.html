Easy Recipes for Uneasy Stomaches

Navigating through nausea can be challenging, but our curated selection of gentle and comforting recipes is here to offer relief. From light, easily digestible meals to soothing beverages, each recipe is designed to provide nourishment and aid in alleviating nausea, making your journey to feeling better a little more manageable and a lot more flavorful.

Ginger + Lemon Tea

This soothing tea is a gentle remedy for calming nausea, particularly effective for soothing an upset stomach. Ginger is known for its anti-nausea properties, easing digestive discomfort, while lemon adds a refreshing touch and aids in settling the stomach.

Ginger + Lemon Tea
Ingredients:
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 2 tsps honey
  • 1 sprig of fresh mint
Directions:
  1. In a small pot, add water, ginger, and lemon juice
  2. Bring to a boil, then turn the heat down and simmer for 10 minutes
  3. Take off the heat, add the honey and fresh mint let cool for 2 minutes
  4. Pour the tea into a mug and enjoy!

Tumeric + Ginger Sweet Potatoes

These baked sweet potatoes infused with ginger and turmeric offer a comforting and nutritious option, combining flavors that are gentle on the stomach while providing anti-inflammatory benefits.

Turmeric + Ginger Sweet Potatoes
Ingredients:
  • 1 large sweet potato
  • 2 tsps ground turmeric
  • 2 tsps ground ginger
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
Directions:
  1. Wash, peel, and cube the sweet potato
  2. Place the cubed potato on a baking sheet and drizzle with olive oil. Season with turmeric, ginger, and salt and pepper
  3. Bake in the oven for 20 minutes at 350 degrees
  4. Let cool before serving and enjoy!

Mashed Banana Rice Pudding

This comforting and gentle rice pudding infused with mashed banana provides a stomach-soothing solution, offering a blend of familiar flavors and ease of digestion.

Mashed Banana Rice Pudding
Ingredients:
  • 2 cups cooked rice
  • 2 cups milk
  • 1 1/2 cups bananas, mashed
  • 2 tsps sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt
Directions:
  1. Add all ingredients to a medium sauce pan and set to medium heat
  2. Bring to a boil and stir occasionally for 15 minutes
  3. Turn off the heat and pur into a bowl
  4. Top with more banana and enjoy!

Ingredient Spotlight

GINGER

Ginger has long been hailed as a natural remedy for nausea due to its potent compounds, notably gingerol. This bioactive substance within ginger helps soothe the stomach, reduce inflammation, and relax gastrointestinal muscles, providing effective relief from nausea and vomiting. Whether consumed as ginger tea, in cooked dishes, or as supplements, ginger's ability to ease digestive discomfort and calm an upset stomach has made it a trusted go-to remedy for generations.
